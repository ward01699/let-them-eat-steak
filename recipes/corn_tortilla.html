Homemade Corn Tortillas

Home About Recipes

Ingredients (Makes ~12 Tortillas)

  • 2 cups masa harina (like Maseca)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (plus more as needed)
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon lard or neutral oil (for softer tortillas)

Instructions

  1. Mix the Dough: Combine masa harina and salt. Slowly add warm water and mix by hand. Add lard or oil if using. Dough should feel soft like Play-Doh.
  2. Rest the Dough: Cover and rest for 20–30 minutes to hydrate.
  3. Divide and Shape: Form 12 golf ball-sized dough balls. Keep covered.
  4. Press the Tortillas: Use a tortilla press with plastic or parchment to flatten each ball into a 5–6 inch circle.
  5. Cook the Tortillas: Cook on a hot dry skillet or comal. 45 seconds on side one, flip, 45 seconds more, then flip again and cook 10–15 more seconds until puffed.
  6. Keep Warm: Stack cooked tortillas in a towel or tortilla warmer to keep warm and soft.

Pro Tips from Lena

  • Use fresh masa harina — it makes all the difference.
  • Don’t skip the dough resting step — it ensures tenderness.
  • That little puff? That’s how you know you nailed it.
  • Best served hot with carne asada, carnitas, or just butter and salt.
